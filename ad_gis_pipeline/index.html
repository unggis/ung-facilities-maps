The University of North Georgia maintains a GIS database of every assignable room across its five campuses. The AD → GIS Pipeline is a weekly process that matches each employee's Active Directory record to a physical room polygon — placing them on a map.

This dashboard tracks the placement rate over time: how many “mappable” employees (those who should have a room assignment) have been successfully located, what is in the gap, and what it would take to reach 100%. A successful placement does more than put a name on a room — it also ties that space to the correct department and division, making the map a live picture of how the institution actually occupies its buildings.

Gaps can have several causes: an employee’s record in Active Directory may be missing room attributes or may have incorrect ones; the GIS polygon database may not yet reflect a recent move or renovation; or the physical space itself may be assigned differently than either system reflects. Each week’s run helps surface these discrepancies so they can be corrected at the source.
